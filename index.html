Our Mission
"Making professional geospatial skills accessible to every Cambodian learner — at an affordable price."

GIS education in Cambodia has traditionally been limited to expensive software, desktop workflows, and a handful of institutions. The Island GIS was created to change that — to give Cambodian students and professionals access to the same tools and methods used by researchers and geospatial engineers globally.

We believe the gap between a Cambodian GIS student and a global geospatial professional is not talent. It is access, language, and the right kind of teaching.

The Island GIS Philosophy
Three ideas that shape
everything we teach
01
Learn Modern, Think Spatial
Traditional GIS taught you where things are. Modern geospatial analysis asks why — and how they change over time. We teach Python and GEE not as replacements for ArcGIS, but as the tools that let you answer deeper questions with the same spatial instincts you already have.
02
Teach Yourself How to Learn
The geospatial field changes fast. Our job is not to give you every answer — it is to teach you how to find answers yourself. How to read documentation. How to debug your own code. How to frame a research question. Skills that outlast any single course.
03
AI Amplifies, You Lead
We live in an age of AI tools that can write code, explain concepts, and accelerate analysis. We teach you how to use them well — which means understanding the outputs, catching the errors, and knowing when the AI is wrong. You lead. AI assists.

About The Island GIS

Built for Cambodian
GIS Learners

The Island GIS was created with one conviction: that the gap between a Cambodian GIS student and a global geospatial professional is not talent — it is access, language, and the right kind of teaching.

The Story
"GIS in Cambodia is mostly still desktop, mostly still English, and mostly still expensive."

That observation sits at the heart of why The Island GIS exists. After working across humanitarian, government, and research sectors in Cambodia, it became clear that the tools and methods that matter most in modern geospatial work — Python, Google Earth Engine, cloud analysis, machine learning — were barely taught locally, and almost never in Khmer.

The Island GIS was built to close that gap. Not by lowering the standard, but by making the standard achievable — through live teaching in Khmer, research-grounded content, affordable pricing, and a philosophy that puts the learner's long-term independence above short-term completion.

Why "The Island"?
An island is a place you can orient yourself from. It sits in the middle of something vast — like the ocean of geospatial knowledge — and gives you a foundation to explore outward. The Island GIS is meant to be that foundation: a place you start from, not where you stay.
What Makes Us Different
Not just what to do — but how to think
🌍
Traditional → Modern GIS
We meet students where they are — experienced with ArcGIS or QGIS — and build from there into Python, GEE, and cloud-scale analysis.
🧠
Teach How to Learn
Beyond the syllabus, we teach students how to read documentation, debug independently, frame questions, and keep building after the course ends.
🔬
Research Literacy
Every course connects to published research. Students learn how to turn real data into a real research output — a skill that serves careers in NGOs, government, and academia alike.
🤖
AI in the Age of GIS
AI tools change what is possible in geospatial work. We teach students to use them well — which starts with understanding the code, not skipping it.
Our Philosophy
Four principles we
will never compromise on
01
Affordable Access
Priced for Cambodian realities. Not internationally — locally. If the price excludes a talented student, it is not a good price.
02
Khmer First
Complex spatial thinking is clearer in your first language. Every live session is in Khmer. Slides and code are in English because that is what employers and research communities use.
03
Depth Over Speed
We do not rush. We would rather teach fewer topics deeply than cover everything superficially. A student who truly understands LULC classification is more valuable than one who has run every GEE function once.
04
Independence as the Goal
The best outcome is not that students keep coming back for courses. It is that they can learn the next thing on their own, apply it to their own problems, and teach someone else.
